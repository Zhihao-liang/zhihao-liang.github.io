Interest in It

Where it started

When I was in primary school, I had an information technology course. In the course, I learned scratch which is a free programming language targeted at children. I still remembered the cute cat logo. With the help of my IT teacher, I wrote a simple game called Greedy Snake. When I saw the snake moving properly, I got a sense of achievement. Scratch made me understand that information technology can create unlimited possibilities. When I was in middle school, I attended an IT club. There are many smart members in the club. I was exposed to more IT knowledge. I learnt C and made a C version of Greedy Snake. I also studied simple HTML and tried to make a personal blog website. Although there is only one class (45 minutes) per week for IT club activities, the experience in the IT club still sparked my interest in IT and pushed me to major in computer science at university.

Why RMIT

There are many reasons to choose RMIT. First, RMIT has a good ranking amongst universities and its subject ranking of computer science is also high. So, I can communicate with more great professors and make more outstanding friends in this outstanding university. Second, RMIT has a great reputation in the IT industry. A number of schoolfellows worked in famous IT companies such as Microsoft, Google, Amazon and so on. This means that it is not hard to get a good job after graduation. Third, the buildings of RMIT are amazing. I enjoy roaming around the big campus.

Expectations

I expect to learn various areas of knowledge in IT such as machine learning, data science, web application development and so on. I would like to broaden my IT knowledge and try to be a full-stack developer in the future. Ideally, I can use Pytorch to do deep learning tasks, use Vue.js to build a user interface efficiently, and use spider technology such as Scrapy to obtain data on the Internet. In the future, I would like to be a Java back-end engineer as my first job.
